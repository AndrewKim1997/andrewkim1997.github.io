Research

My research focuses on trustworthy machine learning, with an emphasis on explainability, uncertainty, fairness, governance, and responsible AI.

Research Overview

I am interested in developing machine learning methods that are not only accurate, but also transparent, reliable, fair, and accountable. My work is broadly motivated by high-stakes decision-making settings, where model predictions can affect individuals and institutions in meaningful ways.

I am particularly interested in how explanation, uncertainty, and fairness can be integrated into the design and evaluation of machine learning systems. Rather than treating interpretability as an afterthought, I view it as part of a broader question of how AI systems should be understood, trusted, and governed.

Research Themes

Explainable AI

I study how machine learning decisions can be explained in ways that are faithful, useful, and appropriate for different users and decision contexts. This includes interests in feature attribution, counterfactual reasoning, explanation evaluation, and the limitations of post-hoc interpretability methods.

Uncertainty Quantification

I am interested in how uncertainty can be represented and communicated in machine learning systems. This includes uncertainty in model predictions, explanations, evaluation procedures, and decision-support settings where a single deterministic output may hide important variation.

ML Fairness

I examine how machine learning systems can produce unequal burdens, errors, or outcomes across individuals and groups. My interests include fairness evaluation, individual-level harms, error analysis, and the relationship between fairness metrics and real-world decision impact.

AI Governance and AI Ethics

I am interested in the broader governance and ethical implications of machine learning systems, especially when they are deployed in sensitive or institutionally important settings. This includes questions of accountability, transparency, contestability, and responsible use.

Interdisciplinary Research Approach

I approach trustworthy AI as an interdisciplinary challenge that connects Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, and the social and institutional contexts in which AI systems are used. I am interested in how technical performance, uncertainty, fairness, ethics, and governance can be studied together rather than treated as separate issues.

My goal is to develop AI and ML methods that are theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and practically meaningful for responsible decision-making. I aim to contribute to transparent, reliable, fair, and accountable intelligent systems across a wide range of real-world settings.
