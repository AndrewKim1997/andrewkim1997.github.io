Research Themes

Explainable AI

I study how machine learning decisions can be explained in faithful, useful, and context-sensitive ways. My interests include feature attribution, counterfactual reasoning, explanation evaluation, and the limitations of post-hoc interpretability methods.

Uncertainty Quantification

I am interested in how uncertainty appears in predictions, explanations, evaluations, and decision-support systems. I focus on cases where a single deterministic output may hide important variation or instability.

ML Fairness

I examine how machine learning systems can produce unequal burdens, errors, or outcomes across individuals and groups. My interests include fairness evaluation, individual-level harms, error analysis, and the relationship between fairness metrics and real-world decision impact.

AI Governance and AI Ethics

I study the broader governance and ethical implications of machine learning systems, especially when they are deployed in sensitive or institutionally important settings. This includes accountability, transparency, contestability, and responsible use.

Core Research Questions

1

How can machine learning explanations be evaluated beyond surface-level plausibility?

2

How should uncertainty be represented when explanations, predictions, or decisions are not stable?

3

How can fairness evaluation account for individual burdens and real-world decision impact?

4

How should AI systems be governed when they are used in institutionally sensitive decision-making settings?

Research Philosophy

I approach trustworthy AI as an interdisciplinary challenge that connects Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, and the social and institutional contexts in which AI systems are used. I am interested in how technical performance, uncertainty, fairness, ethics, and governance can be studied together rather than treated as separate issues.

My goal is to develop AI and ML methods that are theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and practically meaningful for responsible decision-making. I aim to contribute to transparent, reliable, fair, and accountable intelligent systems across a wide range of real-world settings.
